Out Of Bounds

This game was my first ever. It is something I hope I can return to one day, but it functions well enough until then. 

Play as an orphan who was taken under the wing of a deity in return for protecting its tombs across the globe: from the cavernous Gem Forest to the ancient Desert Crypts to even the Old Castle. Traverse  out of bounds to follow the invaders and silence them with your traps. 

Peace-Bringer

This project served as my first major 3D game. Unfortunate significant setbacks proved problematic, but this is another project I am very much anticipating a return to. 

Discover a dark street in limbo as you chase an ethereal being, trying to piece together its past and, eventually, your own. Why are you here? And for what purpose?

Losing Track 

Losing Track is the first major group project I worked on. Created by a team of six, it was a fun discovery of roles within teams and the sorts of creativity that can be generated from teamwork. 

As the driver of a train ferrying souls on the 'fast track' to Avernus, you must tilt the entire vehicle to avoid obstacles on the perilous rails. The faster you go, and the fewer passengers you lose, the higher your score!

Bee-Sieged: War of the Hives

This game serves as an icon of pride. While it may be somewhat simplistic in nature, it provided opportunity for me and my two teammates to develop and market a game in a single process. 

Inspired by the Worms franchise and the Nitrome game Mutiny, this game details the events of the War of the Hives, a devastating battle between dominating wasps and resistance bees. This also marks the first two-player game I have worked on, and will hopefully be developed with a fully-fledged campaign later as it is picked up as a Not Evil Studios project!

Impending Bacon

Another game made during the time of Bee-Sieged, by the same team of three, this served as an entry to a local game jam. 

In this short horror experience, play as a lone maintenance drone aboard the spaceship "SS Laughterhouse". Legends say that the last remaining farm animals lived on this vessel years ago - here's to hoping you don't encounter them now...
